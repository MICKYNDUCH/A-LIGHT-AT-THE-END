LESSON FROM THE STORY OF THE WALL OF JERICHO

Author : Michael. N. Njenga.

The Wall of Jericho was an impenetrable force to any army that was trying to overcome the city. Before even dealing with the wall , the attacking troops would have to navigate their way across the 27feet wide 9 feet deep pit that outline the outer edge of the city. Then there was the wall itself ,17 feet high,5 feet wide pure mass of solid stone. If the attacking troops would manage to get through this without getting shoot down by archers, all that was left was to defeat the well trained Canaanite Army that was waiting within.


This was a reality of a young leader named Joshua and his rag-tag Israelites Army, this Israelites who had seen an entire generation live and die while wondering through the desert were tired, hungry and face what seemed like an impossible task A HOPELESS SITUATION. Now I haven't lived long, but I have lived long enough to know this, that running into Walls is a part of life , and the bigger the wall, the more hopeless the situation can seem. The large wall of Sickness, Loss, Divorce they pop-up around us and they seem Impenetrable and just like the wall at Jericho, this walls rarely stand alone. The wall of our life are often covered with deep pain of Suffering and Despair and an army of hopelessness covers us on every side.

The question is asked all the time. If GOD loves us so much and is so powerful ,then why does he allow this walls to pop-up around me?. Now I don't pretend to have the answers to any of this questions nor do I pretend to know how deep your suffering has been, all I simply want to suggest is ,Maybe God does his work from seemingly hopeless situation ,When I read the bible ,i read about people and characters who all faced a seemingly hopeless situation. In fact,I cannot find a single person who walked faithful with God without first facing a hopeless situation.

  • Adam and Eve ruined paradise by inviting sin into a perfect world, A HOPELESS SITUATION.
  • Joseph was betrayed by his brothers and put in prison ,A HOPELESS CASE.
  • Mosses was cornered at the banks of the red sea by the most powerful army in the world, A HOPELESS CASE.
  • Gideon was 300 against 300,000.
  • David stood a cross a valley of a blood thirsty giant.
  • Easter was a woman trying to gain a word with a pride for a king.
  • Daniel’s roommate was a lion.
  • Daniels friends were thrown in an oven.
  • Jonah got stack in a whale.
  • Peter was a Coward.
  • Paul was imprison.
  • The 5000 had no food.
  • Lazarus was dead.
  • Timothy was too young.
  • Abraham was too old.
  • The youngest son was too stupid.
  • The wall of Jericho were too strong.
  • And Jesus was humiliate , hanged on a cross and buried in a tomb , burring all hope of the revolution that was hoped for for thousands of years .

Now everyone was ready to cross the books on this stories, THE END....GAME OVER.

But if there is one thing we learn about the Scriptures, is that we cannot place a period ( . ) where God has place a comma ( , ). because when all other options are worn out ,when circumstances couldn't get worse and when everything else has failed. Get ready because that is exactly where God show up.

BECAUSE……...

  • Joseph became second in command.
  • The Red sea parted.
  • Gideon won without lifting a weapon.
  • Goliath's head was on a plate.
  • Esther spoke and the king listened.
  • Daniel tamed the lions.
  • The oven felt like room temperature.
  • Nineveh repented.
  • Peter became the rock
  • Paul rejoiced.
  • 12 basket-full were left over
  • Lazarus was just kidding
  • Timothy made a church.
  • Abraham made a family.
  • The youngest son came home to a party.
  • The wall of Jericho came tumbling down .

And Jesus Christ pulled of the resurrection by defeating sin and death and the creation that was merged before so many years ago was now restored for all time. With God what seems like a hopeless situation not only Possible, its Favorable.

  1. A trial into a triumph.
  2. A test into a testimony.
  3. A victim into a victory.

His power is made perfect in weakness, so let us rejoice in our trials and stay strong in the hope that we profess, because he who promised is faithful,was faithful and will always be faithful no matter the situation.
